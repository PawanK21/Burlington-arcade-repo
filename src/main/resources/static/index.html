The Burlington Story

Not a mall.
An institution.

Burlington Arcade did not merely precede the modern shopping mall — it invented it. Opened on 20 March 1819 by Lord George Cavendish, this 196-yard covered arcade in the heart of Mayfair has operated continuously for over two centuries, shaping the very definition of luxury retail.

Patrolled by the Burlington Beadles — Britain's oldest and smallest police force — the Arcade enforces Regency decorum to this day. No whistling. No hurrying. Only Sir Paul McCartney and a world whistling champion have ever been granted exemptions in 205 years.

Britain's oldest private police force in ceremonial uniform since 1819. Whistling banned — a Regency rule against pickpocket signals still enforced today.
